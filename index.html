About Me

Hello! I am Sudarshan Gautam, a researcher specializing in Linguistics, NLP, and multilingual computational frameworks. I began my education in a gurukula, a traditional residential system of learning in India, where I was immersed in classical knowledge under the guidance of my guru. This early education provided a deep understanding of discipline, values, and the holistic approach of traditional scholarship. I hold a BA and MA in Paninian Grammar and have undertaken extensive studies in major branches of Indian philosophy, including Nyāya Darśana, Mīmāṃsā Darśana, and other classical Shastras, developing a strong foundation in logic, linguistics, and analytical reasoning. I am currently pursuing a PhD in Computational Linguistics at the Indian Institute of Technology (IIT), Banaras Hindu University (BHU), Varanasi, focusing on Paninian Grammar. My research integrates insights from classical grammar with modern computational methods, bridging traditional linguistic theory and contemporary language technology.
